Kiro Beauty, a leading beauty makeup brand, wanted to increase Amazon ad-driven revenue while protecting profitability in a highly competitive Beauty & Personal Care category. Growth had started to plateau, so the brief was to lift ad-attributed sales and order volume, scale spend efficiently, and hold the line on ACOS despite rising competition and CPCs.

A review of the prior month showed steady sales but untapped headroom in high-converting keywords and placements; CPC was stable around ₹16, meaning there was room to scale without paying more per click. The plan was a controlled-scaling strategy — deploy more budget into winning campaigns while watching ACOS and CPC daily, rather than cutting spend.

Strategy
  • Increased investment in high-converting Sponsored Products campaigns
  • Expanded coverage on top-performing search terms
  • Improved placement visibility for converting keywords
  • Reallocated budget from low-performing targets to revenue-driving campaigns
  • Monitored ACOS and CPC daily to prevent inefficient scaling
Execution
  • Optimized bids on top-performing keywords and ASIN targets
  • Increased budget allocation for campaigns generating consistent sales
  • Reduced spend leakage from underperforming targets
  • Improved placement participation during peak shopping periods
  • Tracked daily ACOS, CPC, and sales trends to maintain control while scaling
  • Ad-attributed sales grew 55.7% (₹8.83L → ₹13.75L) in a single month of controlled scaling
  • Spend rose only 34.8% (₹9.98L → ₹13.45L) — sales grew faster than cost
  • ROAS improved from 1.67 to 1.75 while ACOS fell from 59.74% to 57.10%
  • CPC held close to ₹16 throughout, confirming the lift came from allocation, not from paying more per click
Key Takeaway

Aggressive scaling and efficiency aren't mutually exclusive. By reallocating budget toward proven keywords and placements and tracking ACOS/CPC daily, sales grew faster than spend — and profitability improved in the same month.

Mahina.Co, a fast-growing female apparel brand based in Gurugram, wanted to aggressively scale revenue while improving advertising efficiency and customer acquisition. Strong products and demand were there, but growth was capped by limited scale across high-intent search terms, rising category competition, and pressure to grow revenue without ACOS climbing.

Account analysis found real headroom in campaign segmentation, keyword ownership, and conversion efficiency — several high-volume search terms lacked visibility, and Sponsored Brands/Sponsored Display were underused, capping both acquisition and remarketing. The plan: a full-funnel Amazon Ads strategy built for growth and profitability together.

Strategy
  • Sponsored Products keyword expansion
  • Exact-match ranking campaigns
  • Sponsored Brand visibility campaigns
  • Sponsored Display retargeting
  • Search term harvesting and keyword isolation
  • Budget allocation based on profitability and growth potential
Execution
  • Rebuilt campaign structures around keyword intent and performance
  • Identified and scaled high-converting search terms
  • Optimized bids using ACOS and conversion data
  • Expanded Sponsored Brand and Sponsored Display coverage
  • Applied negative targeting to reduce wasted spend
  • Shifted budgets toward campaigns delivering profitable growth
  • Generated ₹95.38L in ad-attributed revenue over 365 days (+480% vs prior period)
  • Grew order volume 515% to 29,816 orders
  • Reduced ACOS 24% to a profitable 19.78%, with TACOS holding healthy at 7.43%

Key Takeaway

Full-funnel advertising, keyword ownership, and continuous optimization turned Amazon Ads into Mahina.Co's highest-growth acquisition channel — proving disciplined scaling can deliver both revenue growth and durable marketplace leadership.

Birmingham Newman University, a public university in England, partnered with QS to grow student inquiries and applications for key postgraduate programmes through Meta Ads — while keeping cost per lead efficient and improving lead quality across a competitive, international higher-education market.

Early analysis showed strong audience potential, but broad targeting was driving volume without always producing enrollment-ready prospects. Audience segmentation, creative relevance, and lead-form design were the real levers for quality — so the strategy balanced scale and qualification rather than chasing volume alone.

Strategy
  • Audience segmentation by programme interest and geography
  • Lookalike audience expansion using historical lead data
  • Creative testing across multiple value propositions
  • Lead form optimization to improve qualification
  • Placement optimization based on conversion quality
Execution
  • Launched multiple audience clusters targeting prospective postgraduate students
  • Tested messaging themes: career advancement, employability, global opportunities
  • Optimized lead forms to capture higher-intent prospects
  • Scaled high-performing creatives and paused underperforming assets
  • Reallocated budgets toward audiences with stronger application intent
  • Generated 3,535 student leads over 8 weeks, ~1,591 of them qualified (45%)
  • Reached over 3.4 million prospective students and 8,790 landing page visits
  • Maintained highly efficient traffic acquisition at £0.04 CPC (1.01% CTR)
  • Built a scalable student-acquisition framework for future enrollment campaigns
Key Takeaway

Audience segmentation, creative testing, and lead-qualification work let the campaign scale lead volume and lead quality together — building a repeatable student-acquisition framework for future enrollment cycles.

Vtiger CRM, a SaaS CRM platform based in Bangalore, needed better lead quality, lower acquisition costs, and a scalable paid-acquisition engine. Existing campaigns produced leads, but MQL quality was inconsistent, acquisition costs were climbing, and there was limited visibility into funnel health.

An audit found high-performing keywords mixed in with low-intent search terms, wasting spend and dragging down lead quality, while landing pages were full of distractions that hurt conversion. The fix was a full-funnel optimization framework spanning ads, landing pages, and CRM workflows to tighten intent matching end to end.

Strategy
  • Restructured Google Search campaigns by keyword intent
  • Isolated high-converting CRM-related search terms
  • Launched conversion-focused landing pages
  • A/B tested creatives, CTAs, and user journeys
  • Expanded geo-targeting in high-performing regions
Execution
  • Segmented campaigns by product, competitor, and high-intent search categories
  • Optimized bids and budgets based on lead quality metrics
  • Created distraction-free landing pages focused on demo requests
  • Built reporting dashboards to monitor CPL, MQLs, conversion rate, and ROI in real time
  • Ran ongoing keyword expansion and negative keyword optimization
  • Increased MQL volume by 45%, generating 350+ qualified leads
  • Reduced CPL by 22% while achieving a 30% lead-to-sale conversion rate
  • Improved alignment between paid media and sales pipeline
  • Enhanced reporting and decision-making through centralized HubSpot + Looker Studio dashboards
Key Takeaway

Combining search-intent optimization, landing page improvements, and real-time tracking turned Vtiger's paid acquisition into a scalable revenue engine — more qualified pipeline, lower cost, and a reporting layer the whole team could trust.
